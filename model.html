Section01

The Original Muscle Icon

The Ford Mustang GT is where American muscle was born and where it continues to be defined. Powered by the legendary 5.0-litre Coyote V8 engine producing 460 horsepower, it delivers the kind of raw, visceral acceleration that turns every straight road into a proving ground. Since its debut in 1964, the GT has remained the benchmark for affordable performance — a car that punches above its price class in every measurable way.

Coupe Convertible V8 Muscle
Section02

Track-Ready DNA

The GT Performance Package unlocks a whole new dimension — MagneRide active suspension dampers, larger Brembo brakes, a strut tower brace, and a limited-slip differential combine to give the GT chassis dynamics that rival purpose-built sports cars costing twice as much. On a back road or a race circuit, the GT communicates every input with precision and confidence that makes you feel invincible behind the wheel.

MagneRide Brembo Brakes LSD
Section03

Modern Technology, Timeless Feel

Inside, the GT wraps you in a driver-first cockpit that blends heritage styling cues with modern technology. Ford's SYNC 4 infotainment sits in a 12-inch touchscreen, while the 12-inch digital instrument cluster lets you configure your display to show exactly what matters. Active valve exhaust means you can wake the neighborhood on weekdays or cruise quietly when it counts — the GT adapts to your mood, not the other way around.

SYNC 4 Digital Dash Active Exhaust
Section04

Six Decades of Legacy

Few cars carry the cultural weight of the Mustang GT. Across six decades it has appeared in films, on racetracks, and in the driveways of millions of passionate owners worldwide. The 7th generation introduces a completely redesigned exterior with retro-inspired fastback proportions, a wider stance, and LED lighting that modernizes the silhouette while honoring the bloodline. It isn't just a car — it's a statement about who you are.

DARK HORSE

₱3,785,000
5.0L V8 Hand-Built 500 HP 418 lb-ft Torque 0–60 in 3.7s 155 mph Top Speed 6-Speed Manual / 10-Speed Auto Track-Tuned Suspension
Section01

Stealthy. Lethal. Distinct.

The Mustang Dark Horse isn't merely a trim level — it's a statement of intent. Born from a desire to create the most performance-focused naturally aspirated production Mustang ever, the Dark Horse features a hand-built 5.0-litre Coyote V8 that is individually assembled and cold-tested before installation. Each engine is signed by the technician who built it — a mark of craftsmanship that is increasingly rare in modern automotive production.

Hand-Built Engine Signed Block Track Edition
Section02

Blacked-Out Aggression

The Dark Horse's visual identity is built on contrast and shadow. Unique matte black exterior accents — grille, hood vents, mirror caps, rear diffuser, and spoiler — create a deliberately sinister aesthetic that sets it apart from every other Mustang on the road. Exclusive Dark Horse badging, a unique interior color scheme, and available carbon fiber components complete a package that looks as fast as its numbers suggest.

Matte Black Package Carbon Fiber Options Exclusive Badging
Section03

Engineered for the Circuit

Stiffer springs, revised damper tuning, larger front sway bars, and a unique rear subframe reinforcement give the Dark Horse chassis behavior calibrated explicitly for track use. Brembo six-piston front brakes with larger rotors provide fade-resistant stopping power across multiple hot laps. A standard track apps system logs lap times, g-force, and sector data — turning every track day into a data-driven performance session.

Brembo 6-Piston Track Apps Lap Timer
Section04

A Homage Reborn

The Dark Horse nameplate pays tribute to a 1969 Mustang option package that was as mysterious as it was capable. The 2024 revival takes that spirit and amplifies it for a modern era — more power, more technology, more presence. It exists in the space between the standard GT and the full Shelby GT500, filling a gap that performance purists have wanted bridged for years. This is the Mustang for those who know exactly what they want.

1969 Heritage Performance Gap Filler

SHELBY GT500

₱4,080,000
5.2L Supercharged V8 760 HP 625 lb-ft Torque 0–60 Under 3.0s 202 mph Top Speed 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Carbon Fiber Track Pack
Section01

760 Horsepower of Legend

The Shelby GT500 is the most powerful street-legal Ford ever produced. A supercharged 5.2-litre flat-plane-crank V8 — a layout borrowed directly from motorsport — produces 760 horsepower and 625 lb-ft of torque, propelling the GT500 from 0 to 60 mph in under 3 seconds flat. This is supercar territory from an American muscle car — a fact that has sent shockwaves through the performance car world since its reveal.

Flat-Plane V8 Supercharged Most Powerful Ford
Section02

Aerodynamics as Art

Every surface of the GT500 has been shaped in collaboration with Ford Performance's aerodynamics team. The optional Carbon Fiber Track Pack adds a massive swan-neck rear wing, dive planes, a unique front splitter, and a carbon fiber rear diffuser — generating enough downforce to keep the car planted at over 170 mph. The aggressive wide-body stance isn't styling — it houses wider tracks for improved cornering stability at the limit.

Carbon Fiber Track Pack Swan Neck Wing Wide Body
Section03

Carroll Shelby's Spirit Lives

The Shelby name carries weight that no marketing campaign can manufacture. Since Carroll Shelby transformed a standard Mustang into the legendary GT350 in 1965, the name has been synonymous with the absolute pinnacle of Mustang performance. The modern GT500 inherits that DNA — it is built not merely to win drag races, but to be a complete performance machine capable of defeating far more expensive European machinery on any racetrack.

Carroll Shelby Heritage Since 1965
Section04

Limited, Exclusive, Immortal

Production of the Shelby GT500 is intentionally restricted to approximately 2,000 units per model year, ensuring exclusivity and collector value that appreciates over time. The 7-speed Tremec dual-clutch transmission fires shifts in under 100 milliseconds — faster than a human can blink. Carbon ceramic brake rotors, available as an option, withstand punishing lap after lap without fade. The GT500 is not just the fastest Mustang — it is the Mustang that defines what fast means.

2,000 Units/Year Tremec DCT Carbon Ceramic Brakes

BOSS 302

₱3,900,000
5.0L V8 High-Rev 444 HP 380 lb-ft Torque 0–60 in 3.7s 7,500 RPM Redline 6-Speed Manual Only Track Key System
Section01

Born on the Race Circuit

The Boss 302 nameplate is synonymous with the purest form of Mustang motorsport. Originally created in 1969 specifically to race in the SCCA Trans-Am Series against the Camaro Z/28, the Boss 302 was engineered from the ground up for cornering precision and high-rpm performance. The modern revival stays true to that philosophy — a high-revving, naturally aspirated V8 mated exclusively to a six-speed manual transmission, because a Boss 302 has no business with automatic gearboxes.

Trans-Am Heritage Manual Only High Rev
Section02

The Track Key Advantage

The Boss 302's party piece is the Track Key — a second ignition key that unlocks a reconfigured engine management map, altered traction control calibration, and a distinct exhaust note tuned for maximum visceral involvement on track. The shaker hood scoop, a direct nod to the original 1969 model, forces cold air into the intake while serving as one of the most recognisable visual elements in Mustang history. This is a car with character that cannot be manufactured — it must be earned.

Track Key Shaker Hood Revised ECU Map
Section03

Chassis Excellence

Ford Performance engineers recalibrated every aspect of the Boss 302's suspension specifically for circuit use — stiffer springs, revised damper valving, larger front and rear sway bars, and a unique rear axle ratio that keeps the engine in its powerband through fast sweeping corners. The limited-slip differential provides the mechanical grip needed to put all 444 horsepower down cleanly out of tight hairpins. It is a chassis that rewards precision and punishes carelessness in the most educational way possible.

Track Suspension LSD Sport Alignment
Section04

A Racer's Mustang

Unlike the GT500's supercharged drama or the Dark Horse's brooding street presence, the Boss 302 is fundamentally about the driver's connection to machine. It asks more of you — and in return gives you more satisfaction than any other Mustang when you get it right. The 7,500 rpm redline means you live in the top half of the rev range, working through the precise six-speed gearbox with the kind of mechanical involvement that modern paddle-shift systems simply cannot replicate. This is driving in its most honest form.

Driver's Car 7,500 RPM Pure Experience

COBRA R

₱4,300,000
5.4L V8 385 HP 385 lb-ft Torque 0–60 in 3.8s SCCA Race Ready No A/C, No Radio Roll Cage Provision
Section01

No Compromises. None.

The Mustang Cobra R represents the purest possible interpretation of the Mustang concept — everything unnecessary has been removed, and everything that remains serves a singular purpose: performance. Air conditioning was deleted to save weight. The radio was omitted. Sound deadening was stripped away. What remains is a raw, focused, genuinely dangerous performance machine that the SCCA certified for club racing competition right off the showroom floor. The Cobra R is not a car for everyone. It was never meant to be.

Race Ready Weight Stripped No Frills
Section02

Motorsport Architecture

The Cobra R's 5.4-litre V8 is not the most powerful engine in the Mustang lineup — but it is arguably the most purposefully tuned. Dry-sump lubrication, performance camshafts, and a high-flow intake and exhaust system allow it to sustain full power through extended track sessions without the heat-soak issues that plague conventionally lubricated engines. The strengthened transmission and heavy-duty axle shafts are calibrated for the repeated abuse of competitive racing, not the occasional spirited Sunday drive.

Dry Sump Performance Cams Race Transmission
Section03

Roll Cage Provisions Standard

The Cobra R's body structure was engineered with roll cage installation in mind — mounting points are integrated into the unibody, allowing buyers to install a full competition roll cage without structural modification. Oversized Brembo brakes with race-compound pads, an adjustable front and rear suspension, and a unique front splitter with integrated dive planes are standard fitments. The Cobra R arrives from the factory prepared for competition in a way that no other road-legal Mustang has ever matched.

Roll Cage Mounts Adjustable Suspension Race Brembo
Section04

Collector Status: Guaranteed

Every Cobra R production run has sold out immediately and appreciated substantially in the years that followed. The 1993 run of 107 units, the 1995 run of 250, and the 2000 run of 300 are all considered blue-chip collectibles commanding multiples of their original selling prices today. The Cobra R's value as both a racing tool and a financial asset is unique in the Mustang lineup — it is simultaneously the most demanding car to own and the most rewarding to experience at the absolute limit of its potential.

Limited Production Collector Value Appreciating Asset
